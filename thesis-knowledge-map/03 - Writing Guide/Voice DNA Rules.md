# Voice DNA Rules

**Source:** `voice-dna.md`

These rules govern ALL written output in the prospectus. No exceptions.

## Core Principles
1. Write technically and academically, as a PhD would
2. Get to the point. No throat-clearing, no preamble
3. Be specific: numbers, names, concrete details
4. Vary sentence length: mix short punchy lines with longer ones
5. Use physical verbs for abstract processes: "sanded down" not "improved," "bolted on" not "added," "stripped back" not "simplified"
6. Parenthetical asides are good (for editorial commentary, honest reactions, quick tangents)
7. Never pad output to seem more thorough

## Formatting
- Numbers as digits with commas (e.g., 10,834)
- NO em dashes ever. Use commas, periods, colons, semicolons, or parentheses
- Bold sparingly: 1-2 key moments per section
- Code blocks for specific prompts, commands, or tool outputs

## What Your Writing Already Does Well
From your existing chapters:
- "15 billion of them deployed worldwide" — specific, not vague
- "The result is exactly what you'd expect" — natural, confident
- "(to put it mildly) painful" — parenthetical editorial aside
- "bolted together diverse analysis techniques" — physical verb for abstract process
- "That competition forced a reckoning" — vivid
