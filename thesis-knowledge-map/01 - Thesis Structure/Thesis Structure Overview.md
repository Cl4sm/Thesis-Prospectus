# Thesis Structure Overview

The prospectus has two deliverables:
1. A written document (~100 pages as dissertation draft, condensed to ~20 pages for official proposal)
2. A 45-minute defense presentation

## Chapter Map

```
Ch. 1: Introduction (5-8 pages)
  └─ Problem → Challenges → Thesis Statement → Contributions → Background
Ch. 2: Related Work (8-12 pages)
  └─ Static firmware → Dynamic/rehosting → Symbolic exec → AI for security → CRS
Ch. 3: Operation Mango (25-30 pages)
  └─ Adapted from USENIX paper — YOUR paper, can restructure freely
Ch. 4: Artiphishell (25-30 pages)
  └─ ALL NEW TEXT — you led but didn't write the paper
Ch. 5: Proposed Work — AIL-SE (5-10 pages)
  └─ Motivation → Background → Approach → Prelim results → Timeline
```

## Per-Chapter Notes

### [[Chapter 1 - Introduction]]
The frame for everything. Establishes the 3 walls (scalability, autonomy, precision) and shows how your 3 projects attack each one. Already drafted; needs polish.

### [[Chapter 2 - Related Work]]
Consolidate from both papers plus new material for symbolic execution and AI/CRS. Already drafted; needs expansion in AI/CRS section.

### [[Chapter 3 - Mango Chapter]]
Your paper. You can largely restructure the USENIX paper into dissertation format. Expand sections compressed for conference page limits. Already drafted (63k chars), the longest chapter. See [[Mango Chapter Adaptation]].

### [[Chapter 4 - Artiphishell Chapter]]
**The critical chapter.** You led the project but did not write the paper. All text must be original. See [[Artiphishell Writing Strategy]] for detailed guidance.

### [[Chapter 5 - Proposed Work]]
The "official" proposal. Describes [[AIL Symbolic Execution]] and ties all 3 projects together. Already drafted; needs concrete preliminary results and a firm timeline.

## The 20-Page Question

Per SEFCOM wiki: "the third chapter (the proposed work) can be considered the official proposal while the rest is a draft of your dissertation." Two strategies:
- **Option A:** Ch. 5 alone IS the 20-page proposal
- **Option B:** Condense all chapters proportionally

See [[Reference Proposals]] for how Kyle and Marzieh handled this.
