DARPA committed 29.5 million dollars to this competition. Forty-two teams entered from academia and industry. Seven teams reached the finals. The targets are real open-source projects: Linux kernel, SQLite, Jenkins, Apache libraries, systemd. Not toy benchmarks. Sixty-three vulnerabilities were injected across fifty-three challenge projects spanning twenty-eight repositories covering both C and Java. The competition ran for 143 hours with zero human intervention. Every Cyber Reasoning System operated fully autonomously for nearly a week straight. The scale matters because it forced every team to build systems that generalize across multiple languages, vulnerability classes, and project architectures. No single technique handles that breadth. That constraint shaped our entire architectural approach.
